What is a semiconductor?

A semiconductor material has an electrical conductivity value falling between that of a conductor, such as metallic copper, and an insulator, such as glass. Semiconductors are used to make up computer parts. Examples of semiconductors are silicon, germanium, gallium arsenide, and elements near the metalloid staircase on the periodic table.
